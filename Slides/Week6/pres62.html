What is Unsupervised Learning?
Recall that in supervised learning, we guide the algorithm through
the pattern recognition and inference process.
In the case of document classication, we did this by using a smaller
sample of coded data to train a nave Bayes classier to associate
particular words in a document-term matrix with particular classes of
that coding scheme (e.g., a good vs. a bad product review). We then
applied the trained classier to infer the document class of a larger
set of previously uncoded data.

What is Unsupervised Learning?
The goal of unsupervised learning is generally the same: to nd
patterns in the texts.
The dierence, though, is that we dont have any already-coded data
to provide as a training set. The algorithm nds the patterns in an
emergent fashion.

Document Clustering
A popular form of unsupervised learning is document clustering.
The goal is to use a DTM to group together documents that have
similar distributions across terms in the corpus.
There are forms of unsupervised document classication and
supervised forms of document clustering; but in general, classication
tasks tend to be supervised and clustering tasks unsupervised
(Ignatow and Mihalcea 2018:119).

Distances vs. Similarities
Note that Euclidean distance measures a distance and cosine similarity
measures a similarity.
Distances
In a distance metric, larger values indicate more dissimilarity.
Similarities
In a similarity metric, larger values indicate more similarity.
In both cases, however, the metric quanties the space between the
documents in terms of how similar their word distributions are. Clustering
algortihms generally require distance matrices; but luckily, since the cosine
similarity is bound between 0 and 1, its easy enough to convert it to a
distance: 1 - cos().

Clustering Algorithms
Once we have our document-by-document distance matrix, we are ready
to do some clustering.
The goal is to group together those documents that occupy similar
positions in the vector space: that is, those documents with similar word
distributions in the DTM.

Clustering Algorithms
There are many dierent types of clustering algorithms that vary in
complexity.
Today well focus on one of the simpler ones: hierarchical clustering
with the complete-linkage merging criterion.
Well focus on carrying out a document cluster analysis in R using the
factoextra package (Kassambara and Mundt 2017).

Hierarchical Clustering
In hierarchical clusteringspecically agglomerative hierarchical
clusteringeach document starts as its own cluster.
Then, the documents that are closest to one another in the vector space
are grouped together. This process is repeated until, theoretically, all
documents occupy a sort of super cluster.
So, as we merge clusters together iteratively, how do we decide which
clusters are closest to one another? To do this, we use the
complete-linkage criterion.
Instructor: Marshall A. Taylor SOC/MDSC 43919 February 20, 2019 16 / 31
