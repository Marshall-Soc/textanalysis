Introduction
There are several types of mixed method research.
Recall that a mixed method study is one which uses multiple types of
data, data collection strategies, and/or analytic tools within a
single research project (Small 2011).
One type of mixed method study is the “integrative analysis,” where
multiple analytical strategies are adopted to analyze a single source of
data (Small 2011:76).

What is Grounded Theory?
Grounded theory (GT) is a popular method for qualitative research in the
social and behavioral sciences.
At its core, GT is a method for inductively deriving patterns, themes, and
codes in a set of qualitative data in the service of theory construction
(Charmaz 2006; Glaser and Strauss 1967).
The goal of the GT approach is to avoid imposing predeﬁned categories in
the data coding process. With GT, the themes “emerge” through an
iterative process of data collection, data coding, and literature reading.

What is Grounded Theory?
In GT, “[c]oding means naming segments of data with a label that
simultaneously categorizes, summarizes, snf accounts for each piece of
data” (Charmaz 2006:43).
The GT coding process usually involves the following: initial coding,
focused coding, axial coding, and theoretical coding (Charmaz
2006:47-66).

Issues with GT
Some potential problems:
1
“‘Subjectivities,’ ‘predispositions,’ and ‘personalities’” (Salda˜na
2015:8, quoted in Nelson 2017:3)
2
Philosophically problematic for generating new theoretical insights
(Timmermans and Tavory 2012)
3
Validation and replication (Biernacki 2012, cited in Nelson 2017)
4
“Problem of excess” (Chakrabarti and Frye 2017)

Leveraging Computational Methods
Nelson proposes CGT as a means to address these shortcomings.
With CGT, the research and theory-building process unfolds in three steps:
1
Pattern detection
2
Pattern reﬁnement
3
Pattern conﬁrmation
We’ll talk brieﬂy about each in turn.

Conclusion
CGT is a promising framework for integrating computational and
qualitative forms of text analysis into your research design.
Utility of computational methods: sift through data, ﬁnd patterns, conﬁrm
deductively-derived hypotheses.
Utility of qualitative methods: account for polysemy, assess construct
validity of initial pattern interpretations, ﬁnd results driven by the
computational model and not by the texts themselves.
