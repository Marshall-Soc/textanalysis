Mixed Method Designs
If the text analysis toolkit we have developed so far this semester can
go some way toward addressing our research questions but not
entirely, we may employ a mixed method design.
A mixed method study is one which combines multiple types of
data, multiple types of data collection strategies, and/or
multiple analytic strategies to address a research question or set of
interrelated research questions (Small 2011).

Data Collection
Mixed methods designs can also include multiple types of data collection.
Studies that use more than one data collection strategy are often in the
service of acquiring multiple types of data (like Hanna 2013), but not
necessarily.
Sometimes we need to combine texts from across diﬀerent sources to do
what we need to do.
Case in point: the diﬀusion of discourse.

Analysis Strategies
Finally, one may use multiple types of analytical tools to analyze data.
This can involve combining multiple types of text analysis (like Bail 2012,
2015) or analytical strategies that leverage diﬀerent types of data (like
Hanna 2013).
These studies can also be what Small (2011:76) calls “integrative
analyses,” wherein researchers use two or more tools to analyze one
dataset in an eﬀort to uncover patterns not decipherable by one tool alone.
